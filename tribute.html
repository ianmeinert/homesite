A Tribute to our military heritage

The following pages tell the story of our extended families military heritage. This book is dedicated to those men and women who have served and are still serving, making the sacrifice that many others can not or will not make. Bless them and their families. Semper Fidelis.

"Our debt to the heroic men and valiant women in the service of our country can never be repaid. They have earned our undying gratitude. America will never forget their sacrifices.+ - President Harry S. Truman

GySgt Ian Meinert, USMC

Ian first enlisted in the Marine Corps in March 1998 and later transferred to the reserves. His duty has taken him to Virgina, Ohio, Hawaii, Japan, Thailand, Okinawa, Iraq, Colorado, and Afghanistan. While deployed to Afghanistan, Ian was responsible for Information Systems and logistics, which later allowed him to relocate the Command Post to another region and to also establish the first and only secure communication path between two geographically separated compounds. This would later contribute to uncovering corruption and criminality in the region.

SSgt Richard (Tom) Meinert, USMC

Tom enlisted in the Marine Corps in September 1995 and is still in. His tours have taken him to Okinawa, Hawaii, North Carolina, Michigan, Iraq, Kuwait, and California.

SPC Charles Palmer, USA

Stationed in Germany as a tank mechanic during the Vietnam War.

Col James Dennany, USAF

James served during Vietnam. In the last month before Jim was to rotate home, he was scheduled to fly as a Weapons Systems Officer in an escort mission for an AC-130 Gunship over a particularly dangerous area. To protect the Gunship, he attacked areas of heavy ground fire; unfortunately, his F4D sustained serious damage and crashed. Other aircrews described it as "a big ball of fire" on impact, so there was little chance for a crew to survive. He gave his all to protect a number of crew members on the AC-130 gunship, allowing them to complete their mission and survive. Just weeks before his fateful mission, James had been awarded the Distinguished Flying Cross for his bravery during earlier missions. In the absence of proof of death he was classed as missing in action; later being repatrioted in January 2011.

PFC Robert A. Walker, USMC

While Robert was assigned to D Company, 3rd Marines in 1963, he performed a 10 week training evolution on Mt. Fuji, Japan. The 10 weeks consisted of live firing and cold weather training. The Third Marines were the ground element of the Seventh Fleet in Okinawa.

PFC Leo J. Dennany, USMC

Leo enisted in the Marine Corps in 1949. A year later, he would be deployed to the conflict in Korea sometime in 1950. He served for four years, being discharged honorably in 1953.

Capt Jack Miller, USAF

In the early stages of World War II, Jack was working with the China Air Company when he was called back to the states. Over the next few years, he trained our pilots as a Civilian Pilot Instructor in Oklahoma. Later on, he would writing the rules for pilots (FAA regs), pretty much forcing himself out of the pilots seat and into retirement! His passion for flying kept him in the air for 80 years.

PVT Lynton Palmer, USA

Lynton joined the Army after high school (born in 1913), serving until 1945.
